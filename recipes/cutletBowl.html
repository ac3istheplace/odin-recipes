Katsudon (The Pork Cutlet Bowl)

Description

What is katsudon? You may be wondering why you clicked for a pork cutlet bowl recipe, and now see the word katsudon. That's because they're the same! Katsudon (pronounced cot-soo-doan) is the Japanese name of this dish. It's a (normally pork) cutlet coated in breadcrumbs and deep fried, on a bed of rice and egg, with onions and a sweet and savory sauce!

Let's get into the recipe, starting with what ingredients you need. This recipe makes 2 bowls, so be sure to adjust ingredient amounts as needed!

If you do not have leftover tonkatsu, follow my recipe here, as you need pre-cooked pork for this recipe!

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup dashi stock
  • 2 teaspoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sake
  • 1/4 teaspoon potato starch
  • 2 teaspoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 thin tonkatsu (pork cutlets)
  • 1 scallion (optional garnish)
  • 2 bowls of cooked rice (white suggested)

Steps

  1. Break the eggs (3) into a bowl and partially mix until yolks are broken but still somewhat separate from the whites.
  2. Add the dashi (1/3 cup), sugar (2 tsp), soy sauce (1 tbsp), sake (1 tbsp), and potato starch (1/4 tsp) to a bowl and mix until combined.
  3. Add the oil (2 tsp) and onion (1/2 small) to a non-stick frying pan on medium-high heat. Saute the onions until soft and beginning to brown.
  4. Push onions to edge of pan and add the tonkotsu (2) in the center.
  5. Pour the sauce from step 2 on/around the cutlet and cover for 1 minute to let sauce thicken (and the katsu reheat if left over).
  6. Remove the lid and flip the katsu over. Pour egg all over/around the katsu and sprinkle on the green onions . Cover and steam until the egg is a little less cooked than you want. By the time it gets to the table, the residual heat cooks it the rest of the way.
  7. Set your bowls by laying a bed of the cooked white rice (follow instructions on rice package to cook properly). Cover rice with the tonkatsu/egg mix from your pan, and add any remaining sauce on top.
