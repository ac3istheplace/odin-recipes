Katsudon (The Pork Cutlet Bowl)

Description

What is katsudon? You may be wondering why you clicked for a pork cutlet bowl recipe, and now see the word katsudon. That's because they're the same! Katsudon (pronounced cot-soo-doan) is the Japanese name of this dish. It's a (normally pork) cutlet coated in breadcrumbs and deep fried, on a bed of rice and egg, with onions and a sweet and savory sauce!

Let's get into the recipe, starting with what ingredients you need. This recipe makes 2 bowls, so be sure to adjust ingredient amounts as needed!

Ingredients

  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup dashi stock
  • 2 teaspoons granulated sugar
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sake
  • 1/2 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 thin tonkatsu (pork cutlets)
  • 1 scallion (optional garnish)
  • 2 bowls of cooked rice (white suggested)
